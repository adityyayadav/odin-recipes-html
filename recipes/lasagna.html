LASAGNA

Description

Lasagna is an Italian pasta dish that features wide,
flat sheets of pasta layered with fillings such as a meat sauce (ragù), béchamel sauce, vegetables,
and various cheeses like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan.

The assembled dish is typically baked in an oven until hot and bubbly,
with grated cheese on top. Lasagna can also refer specifically to the wide, flat noodle itself.

Core Incredients

  • Noodles
  • Meat
  • Sauce
  • Cheese
  • Binder
  • Seasonings

Steps To Prepare:

  1. Prepare Ingredients: Make the meat sauce (brown meat, add sauce, simmer) and the cheese mixture (combine ricotta, egg, and seasonings).
  2. Cook Noodles: Boil lasagna noodles until al dente (firm), then drain and set aside. (You can skip this step if using "oven-ready" or fresh noodles).
  3. Assemble Layers: In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce on the bottom to prevent sticking. Build layers in this order: noodles, cheese mixture, meat sauce, and mozzarella cheese.
    Repeat the layers until the ingredients are used, finishing with a final layer of noodles topped with sauce and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  4. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil and bake in a preheated oven (usually 350-375°F or 175-190°C) for about 45 minutes.
  5. Finish & Rest: Uncover the lasagna and bake for another 10-15 minutes until the cheese is golden and bubbly. Let it rest for at least 10-15 minutes before slicing to help it set.
